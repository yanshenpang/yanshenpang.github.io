Data Analysis -Telco Customer Churn Analysis (Part 2 )

Introduction

The telecommunication industry has brought the world closer together by enabling seamless communications, data exchange and access to critical services. Like other companies in different industries such as finance and manufacturing, the telecommunication industry also has its own sets of business challenges. Globalisation and advancement in communication technology have brought more alternatives to customers. This competitive landscape in the industry presents a lower barrier for customers to switch from one provider to another provider. This gives rise to a significant challenge in the industry, customer churn. Customer churn refers to the rate at which customers stop using the products or services of the company.

Problem Statement

Databel is a fictitious telecommunication company that is headquartered in the United States. Over the years, the company has grown to be one of the major players in the telco industry, serving customers across the United States and diverse customer demographics and age groups. The company achieved a total revenue of around 7.2 million USD with 6,682 customers in the previous financial year. The company faced a customer churn rate of around 26.9%, accounting for around 20% of the total revenue. Persistent customer churn problems will result in a financial impact as the company will lose the recurring income generated by its customers. The company will face the risk of losing more revenue in the next financial year if the company does not address this challenge efficiently. The company might also face the risk of losing market share to its competitors and this will hinder the companyâ€™s growth. The company can choose to acquire new customers to counteract the impact of customer churn. However, this approach is not without its challenges. Recruiting new customers usually can increase the cost by five to twenty-five times more than retaining existing customers. The high cost and complexity of the acquisitions of new customers can also be due to the negative effects of word of mouth from churned customers. Therefore, it is important to predict customer churn in the company. By identifying customers with a high chance of churning, the company can derive effective and tailored strategies to retain customers.

The aim of this study is to build a customer churn predictive model that could identify if a customer will churn in the telecommunication industry so that the company can derive a tailored strategy to retain the customer and reduce customer churn.


Objectives

1. To predict customers customer churn in the telecommunication company using machine learning models.

2. To determine the factors contributing to customer churn in the company using feature importance.

3. To provide insights for the telecommunication company to reduce customer churn rate and strategize customer retention plan.

Scope

A fictitious telecommunication company dataset will be used in this study and the dataset can be obtained from Kaggle at https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/yichienchong/databel-telecom-customer-churn-dataset . The dataset will consist of 6,687 observations with around 29 variables. The variables include details of the customers and factors that might be related to the customer churn in the telecommunication company. Two models which are decision tree and logistic regression models will be created using the SAS Enterprise Miner . The models will be built with variations and different construction and optimisation properties. The models will then be evaluated and the best model will be used to solve the problem statement as mentioned in the business case. The last part of the study will conclude with recommendations for the telecommunication company to address the customer churn problem using the core findings obtained from the predictive model.


Methodology

This study will be using CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) for the methodology. The six different phases in CRISP-DM are business understanding, data understanding, data preparation, modelling, evaluation and deployment. In the first step, the problem in the telecommunication business will be defined and understood first. In the data understanding step, the relevant data related to customer churn in telecommunication companies will be gathered from the Kaggle website. The dataset will be explored to understand the structure, size and types of variables. Exploratory Data Analysis will also be done on the dataset to understand the data quality and missing values in the dataset. Next, data preparation will be conducted on the dataset to handle missing values, data transformation and one-hot encoding. Feature engineering will also be done in this step to prepare relevant variables for model creation. For modelling, appropriate machine learning models will be built to predict customer churns. In this study, two different models are decision tree-based model and the regression-based model will be built to predict the customer churn in the telecommunication company. Models with various optimizations and variations will be built. In the evaluation step, the performance of the predictive models will be compared using evaluation metrics such as misclassification rate, recall and precision. Lastly, in the deployment step, the output from the chosen predictive model will be analysed and interpreted. The analysis will be used to derive recommendations that can help the company retain customers and reduce customer churn.
